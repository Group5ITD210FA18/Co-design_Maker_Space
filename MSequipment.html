Equipment

3D Printers

3D printers deposit material that solidifies into three-dimensional objects by utilizing a plotting mechanism that is controlled by a computer following a CAD generated model. A quinessential makerspace technology, 3D printers are used in both rapid prototyping and additive manufacturing. The several 3D printers in the lab should be of interest to any applied science students looking to gain skills in this important and emerging industrial technology area.

Servo Setup

The Lab has a servo setup to introduce students to the study of more complex robotics. The Lab's set includes apparatus for testing motor control and sensors, and a base and coordinate parts of a robotic arm that can be programmed to mimic the actions of a human arm in a robotic manufacturing setting.

Laser Cutter

The Lab has two laser cutters available for student use. Laser Cutting is a non-contact process which utilizes a laser to cut materials, resulting in dimensionally accurate cuts. The process works by directing the laser beam through a nozzle to the workpiece. A combination of heat and pressure creates the cutting action that results in an edge with a high-quality surface finish.

CAD Plotter

The Lab includes a drum plotter printer designed for printing the long, continuous, point-to-point lines required of the exact reproduction of vector graphic drawings created on CAD systems. Plotters are ideal for printing large-scale architectural blueprints, engineering designs, and other CAD applications up to 44 inches wide.

Raspberry Pi Boards

The Lab's Raspberry Pi resources should be of interest to computer science students looking to gain hands-on practice in the design of technologies that can utilize their Scratch and Python programming skills. Raspberry Pi boards are small format circuit boards that can be plugged directly into the lab's computer monitors and programmed using by a standard keyboard and mouse inputs.

Wacom Tablet

The Lab has several computer input devices available to student designers, including a Wacom drawing tablet. The tablet/stylus interface is extremely useful in design stage. Designers find that the set-up facilitates direct interaction with a computer graphic - similar to the experience of sketching on paper, and is much more intuitive than manipulating a computer graphic by keyboard or a mouse.
